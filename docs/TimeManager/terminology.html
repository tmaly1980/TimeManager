  • Gantt Chart:
    A timeline-based chart involving tasks, milestones, and or projects visually displaying their current progress, compared to their estimated timeline.
  • Groups:
    A group is a team of workers who have a common purpose such as "marketing" or "software devlopment". A group typically has a manager. People can belong to any number of groups.
  • Manager:
    Someone who has the authority to say what tasks you should do, and when. They typically lead an entire team of workers, in a group.
  • Milestone:
    A large accomplishment within a project, fulfilling a defined goal. They are a specialized form of task groups meant for projects, and they contain any number of tasks that as a whole accomplish this goal.
  • Product:
    The items involved with a business that people work on, ultimately being sold to customers.
  • Project:
    A large group of tasks with a common goal, typically involving changes to a product. It includes a timeline, as well as several milestones, or task groups, grouping tasks into overall project goals. It can involve participants from any arbitrary groups/teams within the business organization, such as if a project is interdepartmental.
  • Project Manager:
    Someone who is held responsible for the planning, organization, task distribution, monitoring and ultimately the completion of a project.
  • Site Administrator:
    Someone who has been given full access rights to altering and assigning tasks to any user in the system. They are also capable of creating users, groups, and products in the system's database.
  • Subtask:
    A special kind of task that is a subdivision of the work of another task, called the parent task. It is similar to a task dependency.
  • Task:
    A definite thing that needs to be done. It contains a timeline -- when to start, when to get it done by, and how much time it will take -- as well as a status, priority, and category (type of task).
  • Task Dependency:
    A separate task that is required to be completed prior to another task can be either started or completed. Unlike a subtask, it is a completely separate piece of work from the dependent task.
  • Task Group:
    A logical group of tasks, with a common goal. Tasks can be in any number of task groups.
  • Task Owner:
    Someone who is held responsible for the completion of a task. They typically do all the work involved, but may subdivide the task into subtasks, and distribute those subtasks to other people (who become task owners of those subtasks).
  • Task Requestor:
    Someone who requests that a task be done, typically the group manager of the task's designated owner. Requestors may assign tasks to other people whom they do not manage via a group, if the task owner is allowed to be assigned tasks from the specific requestor.
